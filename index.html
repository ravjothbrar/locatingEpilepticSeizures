Overview

This system predicts the epileptogenic zone (EZ) — the brain region where a seizure originates — from scalp EEG data. It outputs exact 3D coordinates (in MNI space) and a confidence spread, visualised as a glowing hotspot on the interactive brain model.

The Model: Physics-Informed Neural Network

Unlike standard neural networks that only learn patterns from data, a PINN is also constrained by the laws of physics. During training, the model learns to reject predictions that violate the Hodgkin-Huxley equations — the fundamental equations governing how neurons fire.

The 4 Hodgkin-Huxley Equations

These equations describe how electrical signals propagate through neurons via ion channels. They are embedded as constraints in both training and inference:

1. Membrane Voltage C_m · dV/dt = I_ext - g_Na·m³·h·(V-E_Na) - g_K·n&sup4;·(V-E_K) - g_L·(V-E_L)
2. Sodium Activation (m) dm/dt = α_m(V)·(1-m) - β_m(V)·m
3. Sodium Inactivation (h) dh/dt = α_h(V)·(1-h) - β_h(V)·h
4. Potassium Activation (n) dn/dt = α_n(V)·(1-n) - β_n(V)·n

Inference Pipeline

When you click "Run Analysis", the following happens entirely in your browser:

1
PCA Preprocessing
Your 18-channel EEG is decorrelated using principal component analysis, matching the training data's distribution.
2
Forward Pass
The pre-trained PINN runs a forward pass, outputting initial 3D coordinates and Hodgkin-Huxley state variables (V, m, h, n) at each timestep.
3
Physics Optimization (30 steps)
The HH state variables are refined through 30 gradient descent steps, minimising the residual of all 4 HH equations while staying close to the model's initial prediction.
4
PCA Interpretability
The model's internal 128-dimensional latent representation is decomposed via PCA. Each principal component is correlated with the HH variables to reveal what the model learned.

Training Data

The model was trained on the CHB-MIT Scalp EEG Database (PhysioNet) — 5 paediatric patients with annotated seizure onsets. EEG segments were augmented (noise, scaling, channel dropout, time shift) and the EZ ground truth was derived from the energy-weighted centroid of electrodes showing earliest ictal activity.

Limitations

Scalp EEG has ~1-2 cm spatial resolution. The predicted EZ is a probabilistic hotspot, not a surgical-precision pinpoint. This tool is for research and educational purposes only — it is not a medical device. Clinical EZ localisation requires invasive monitoring (SEEG) and expert interpretation.
